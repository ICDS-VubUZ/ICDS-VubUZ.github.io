Master Thesis

ICDS offers general guidance, pointing out possible strategies, and techniques, software and ways of communicating both the design and statistics involved.

Research

ICDS offers a look over your shoulders to make sure appropriate methodology and statistics are used, possibly suggesting better ways, and to help communicating the choices and procedures to peers.

Research Groups

ICDS can be fully involved to take responsibility for the study design and/or data analysis, for writing the statistical section with results and conclusions and dealing with reviewer comments. ICDS also can be involved in optimizing the data collection and processing for recurrent studies and for bringing in the latest statistical techniques.

Consultancy

Statistical and methodological advice is offered to all researchers at the VUB and UZ Brussel, as well as students working on their thesis. Together we can establish the requirements for data collection (eg., sample size) and the possibilities for the analyses. Support is also offered in writing protocols and research proposals.

Data Analysis

For the statistical analysis we can offer to collaborate with researchers at the VUB and UZ Brussel. A collaboration would also make us responsible for communicating the results of a statistical section and responding to remarks made by reviewers.

Tutoring

Workshops and seminars may be provided on a wide variety of topics like sample size calculation, data registration, statistical techniques, and more. Our aim is to enhance the existing proficiency in statistical and methodological reasoning where needed.

Automation

Parts of the data collection, data analysis and reporting can be automated, a process we may be able to set up. Automation avoids repetitive work, errors and time loss, and it promotes uniformity.

Terms of Service

Statistical and methodological support is offered to all researchers of the Life Sciences and Humanities within the VUB / UZ Brussel community.

Consultancy can be offered to all members of our community for design of studies - sample size derivation - choice of statistical techniques - communication of design, analyses and results. Independent researchers and their doctoral students can get advice on, and fully outsource data analysis - dealing with reviewers - writing research proposals - automating data collection and processing - tutoring (workshops, group discussions, ...).

For members of our community (others need to contact the ICDS coordinator)

Consultations are free of charge. The number of consults can be limited by the consultant. A single consult takes up a maximum of one hour. Consults take place at the consultant's office. A consent of the promotor is required for (Phd.) students.

Data Analysis can be free of charge, but their acceptance depends on the available time. Researchers are adviced to request for assistance well in time. For a more involved commitment researchers are adviced to discuss the expected contribution and any compensation in advance, ideally requesting for financial support when applying for funding, in order to guarantee timely help. Co-authorship includes a committment to the communication of methods, statistics and results, as well as to the review process. The outsourcing of data analysis is accepted at the discretion of the consultant.

Reference: Interfaculty Center Data processing & Statistics, Vrije Universiteit Brussel

Tutoring and Automation should be discussed well in time and acceptance is accepted at the discretion of the tutor/analyst.

Practicalities

Data offered to ICDS should be in an appropriate format. Please contact ICDS in advance for assistance if in doubt. Data remains property of the researchers or any other person who delivers the data. Data will be saved in an internal ICDS database. Public use will require consent of an owner of the data.

Ethical Code

The ICDS acts for its statistical service in accordance with the ethical code established by the American Statistical Society.

General Rules

Consultant and client are lead by the principles of professional and scientific integrity. The variety of methodological support is extensive so that no fixed terms nor expectations can be set apriori. Consultants will not be pressured in time nor course of action. The quality of the data defines the statistical potential and are the responsibility of the client. The consultants will provide their service within that potential, offering no guarantee that the most optimal analysis is possible. Consultants can always exert their right to decline or stop an assignment, also based on poor data quality or conclusions fail to meet basic scientific requirements.

Consultants

The degree of involvement of the consultant will be discussed in advance and agreed upon with the client. The consultant aims to offer the most appropriate scientific sollution to succesfully perform the study. The consultant prepares the meeting and takes sufficient time to interact with the client to cover the assignment in full detail, including all issues related to the design of a study, the data and forseable methodological challenges. The consultant informs the client about possible conflicts of interest, ethical limitations and all other aspects that could impact the design, method and analysis. The consultant informs about the statistical background and knowledge of the client and will, in agreement with the client, offer services complementary to the available knowledge. If a client makes suggestions or attempts to perform the analysis, then the consultant will discuss advantages and disadvantages in a way that would meet the background knowledge of the client. If the consultant performs the analyses the client will in the end obtain all relevant details including information on the pre-processing, coding of variables and software details. In preparation of any consecutive scientific communication the consultant will point out conclusions but also limitations and conditions for those conclusions to be valid. The consultant will at the request of the client read through the relevant sections and where necessary suggest improvements.
Note that the consultant will refrain from any analysis when encountering problems with the study design, hypothesis, research questions or data collection. The consultant will offer ways to solve any problems encountered in order to meet statistical 'good practice'. Also note that the consultant only will accept an assignment that is deemed possible, in light of the intended conclusions.

Client

The client tries to specify the project, experiment, hypothesis, research questions, a clearly as possible, including all marginal conditions. The client will therefore also provide a layman introduction of the data collection and nature of the data. Whenever possible, data should be translated to what is statistically of interest. The consultant is typically not an expert in the field of the client so the client should avoid their jargon in as far as possible.
The client offers insight into possible problems with the data collection and measurement process. If the intitial protocol was deviated from, this should be pointed out by the client with explicit information on the causes and how the situation is dealt with. In case of missing data, extreme outliers, or other unexpected observations, they should also be discussed so that the consultant can deal with those in an optimal way. The client will translate the statistical results to their own field of expertise. The client will, together with the consultant, discuss the correct interpretation of results. The client will judge and appreciate the contribution made by the consultant when making scientific reports. If the client considers the contribution of the consultant to be substantial, the client would consider co-authorship for the consultant when agreed upon. When not included as a co-author, because the contribution is minimal, all resulting scientific reports should mention that ICDS performed the analysis unless denied by the consultant.

Mission Statement

Methodological and statistical support to help make a difference

ICDS provides complementary support in methodology and statistics to our research community, for both individual researchers and research groups, in order to get the best out of them.

ICDS aims to address all questions related to quantitative research, and to further enhance the quality of both the research and how it is communicated.

Workshop

Sample Size Calculation with GPower

Edited and extended version of a workshop on sample size calculations using GPower designed for the doctoral school. The necessary concepts are introduced, covering power, effect size and type I/II errors. Small illustrative exercises are included to provide a first experience with GPower as well as to touch on relevant statistical reasoning. While focusing mainly on the comparison of 2 independent groups to explain the core of sample size calculation, several extensions are highlighted including paired groups, multiple groups, and correlations instead of differences for effect size.

Info Session

Methodological and statistical issues to communicate in a research proposal

Draft highlighting the key ingredients, research aim and research design, that can be part of a research proposal. A brief discussion is included on different types of research aims (confirmatory, exploratory, preparatory, techn(olog)ical) and different aspects of research design (quantity & quality of observation and generalization).
